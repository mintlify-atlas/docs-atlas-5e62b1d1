Get started with Click-n-Color and pick your first color in just a few clicks.

## Pick your first color

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the extension">
    Click the Click-n-Color icon in your browser toolbar. If you don't see it, click the extensions icon (puzzle piece) and pin Click-n-Color.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click 'Pick a Color'">
    In the popup window, click the **Pick a Color** button. The popup will close and your mouse cursor will change to a crosshair.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select a color">
    Move your cursor over any part of the webpage. You'll see a magnifier showing the color under your cursor. Click to select that color.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Color is saved">
    The popup will reopen automatically showing your picked color. The color is:
    - Displayed in the color preview box
    - Shown in your selected format (RGB by default)
    - Automatically copied to your clipboard (if auto-copy is enabled)
    - Saved to your palette
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
The extension captures a screenshot of the visible page to enable precise color picking. This happens instantly when you click "Pick a Color."
</Info>

## Understanding the interface

The Click-n-Color popup has several sections:

### Current color display

At the top of the popup, you'll see:
- **Color preview box** - Visual representation of your selected color
- **Color value** - The color code in your chosen format
- **Format chips** - Switch between RGB, HEX, and HSL formats by clicking the chips
- **Copy button** - Manually copy the color value to clipboard

### Color palette

The main section shows your saved colors:
- Click any saved color to select it and copy to clipboard
- Hover over a color to see the delete button (×)
- Colors are ordered by recency (newest first)
- Up to 50 colors by default (configurable in settings)

### Action buttons

At the bottom:
- **Export Palette** - Export your colors as JSON, CSS Variables, or PNG
- **Settings** - Configure default format, palette size, and auto-copy
- **Themes** - Choose between Dark, Light, or Blue themes

## Working with picked colors

### Switch color formats

Click the format chips (RGB, HEX, HSL) to instantly convert your selected color:

```
RGB: rgb(138, 43, 226)
HEX: #8a2be2
HSL: hsl(271, 76%, 53%)
```

The conversion happens instantly without re-picking the color.

### Copy to clipboard

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Auto-copy (Default)">
    By default, colors are automatically copied when picked. Look for the "Color copied to clipboard!" notification.
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Manual copy">
    Click the **Copy to clipboard** button below the color value to copy at any time.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Delete colors

To remove a color from your palette:
1. Hover over the saved color square
2. Click the **×** button that appears
3. The color is immediately removed

### Clear all colors

To clear your entire palette:
1. Click the **Clear All** button
2. Click **Confirm Clear** when prompted
3. All colors are removed from your palette

<Warning>
The clear operation cannot be undone. Export your palette first if you want to keep a backup.
</Warning>

## Export your palette

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open export dialog">
    Click the **Export Palette** button at the bottom of the popup.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose format">
    Select one of three export formats:
    
    - **JSON** - Array of color values for use in code
    - **CSS Variables** - Custom properties ready to use in stylesheets
    - **PNG Image** - Visual grid of your color palette
  </Step>

  <Step title="Download file">
    The file will download automatically to your default downloads folder.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Export your palette regularly to back up your colors or share them across projects.
</Tip>

## Customize your settings

### Change default format

1. Click **Settings** button
2. Select your preferred format: RGB, HEX, or HSL
3. Click **Save settings**

Your chosen format will be automatically selected when you open the extension.

### Adjust palette size

The default maximum is 50 colors, but you can change it:

1. Click **Settings**
2. Enter a number between 10 and 100 in **Max palette size**
3. Click **Save settings**

When the limit is reached, the oldest colors are automatically removed.

### Toggle auto-copy

1. Click **Settings**
2. Toggle the **Auto-copy on Pick** switch
3. Click **Save settings**

When enabled, colors are automatically copied to clipboard when picked.

## Choose a theme

Click-n-Color includes three beautiful themes:

1. Click the **Themes** button
2. Select **Dark** (default), **Light**, or **Blue**
3. Click **Save theme**

The interface instantly updates with your chosen theme's color scheme.

<Tip>
The Blue theme is highly recommended by the creator for the best visual experience.
</Tip>

## Keyboard shortcuts

- **ESC** - Cancel color picking mode
- **Click** - Pick the color under cursor
- **Mouse move** - Preview color in magnifier
